Did you know? Black Friday's history is deeply connected to Thanksgiving.  Retailers saw the day after Thanksgiving as the perfect opportunity to start the holiday shopping season. 

The term "Black Friday" originally referred to heavy traffic on that day.  In the 1960s, Philadelphia police used it to describe the chaos of pedestrian and vehicular congestion. 

Black Friday didn't become the busiest shopping day of the year until the 2000s.  Cyber Monday only came into existence with the rise of the internet. 

The day after Thanksgiving has been regarded as the beginning of America's Christmas shopping season since 1952, although the term "Black Friday" didn't become widely used until recent decades. 

Black Friday is now known in many parts of the world and has been associated with massive sales and sometimes even scarcity-driven shopping behavior. 

Retailers often open early and offer promotional sales on Black Friday to kick off the holiday season. 

Some stores extend their Black Friday sales online, making it easier for customers to shop without the crowd. 

Its estimated that over 50% of Americans partake in Black Friday shopping, making it one of the busiest days for retailers both in stores and online. 

Fun fact: The earliest known use of "Black Friday" in print was in 1966, published in The American Philatelist, a magazine for stamp collectors.
