Did you know? Black Friday's history is deeply connected to Thanksgiving.  Retailers saw the day after Thanksgiving as the perfect opportunity to start the holiday shopping season. 

The term "Black Friday" originally referred to heavy traffic on that day.  In the 1960s, Philadelphia police used it to describe the chaos of pedestrian and vehicular congestion. 

Black Friday didn't become the busiest shopping day of the year until the 2000s.  Cyber Monday only came into existence with the rise of the internet.
